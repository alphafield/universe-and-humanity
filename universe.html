Universe

The universe is the constraint layer: invariants, admissible transitions, and stability regions. If a claim has no measurable edge—no boundary condition, no conservation, no detectable failure—it is not about the universe.
DECLARATION
We do not name mysteries. We specify invariants.
We do not decorate unknowns. We define what must not change.
We do not confuse explanation with constraint. A constraint survives translation.
U1
Invariants
Quantities that remain stable under permitted transformations: conservation, symmetry, monotones.
U2
Admissible Transitions
What changes are allowed, at what rate, and under what boundary conditions.
U3
Stability Regions
Basins where dynamics self-correct; edges where drift accumulates.
U4
Detectable Failure
Where prediction breaks: divergences, runaway modes, irreversibility, loss of coherence.
INTERFACE CHECK
Input: initial/boundary conditions; sources; constraints.
Coupling: laws linking states; permissible operators; geometry.
Feedback: measurable return signals; residuals; error scaling.
Repair: renormalization, coarse-graining, self-stabilizing mechanisms.
Failure: singularities, instability, non-closure, unbounded error.
